Statistical paradoxes highlight flaws in human intuition regarding probability and data.

### Simpson's Paradox
A trend that appears in different groups of data disappears or reverses when these groups are combined. It emphasizes the importance of confounding variables.

### Berkson's Paradox
A false observation of a negative correlation between two positive traits, arising from selection bias. (e.g., "Why are handsome men jerks?").

### Monty Hall Problem
A classic counter-intuitive probability puzzle based on conditional probability. Staying with an initial choice gives a $1/3$ chance of winning, while switching gives $2/3$. These paradoxes warn against blind reliance on simple [[p-values-effect-size|P-values]].
