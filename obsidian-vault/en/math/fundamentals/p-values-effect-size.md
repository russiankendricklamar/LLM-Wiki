Null Hypothesis Significance Testing (NHST) is standard in science, but heavily misunderstood.

### P-values
A **p-value** is the probability of observing data at least as extreme as the actual data, assuming the null hypothesis is true. It is *not* the probability that the null hypothesis is false. 

### Effect Size
Statistical significance (low p-value) does not mean practical significance. **Effect size** measures the magnitude of the difference. A common metric is **Cohen's d**:
$d = \frac{\mu_1 - \mu_2}{\sigma}$

### P-Hacking
Manipulating data or analyses until non-significant results become significant is called **p-hacking**. This leads to reproducibility crises and highlights the need to understand [[statistical-paradoxes|Statistical Paradoxes]].
