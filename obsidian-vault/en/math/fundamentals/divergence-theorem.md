## Overview
The Divergence Theorem (Gauss's Theorem) relates the flux of a vector field through a closed surface to the divergence of the field in the volume enclosed.

## Mathematical Formulation
Let $V$ be a volume bounded by a closed surface $S$. For a continuously differentiable vector field $\mathbf{F}$:
$$ \iint_S \mathbf{F} \cdot d\mathbf{S} = \iiint_V (\nabla \cdot \mathbf{F}) \, dV $$

It implies that the outward flux is equal to the volume integral of the source density.
