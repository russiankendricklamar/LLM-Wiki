A **random walk** is a stochastic process describing a path of successive random steps.

### 1D and 2D Simple Walks
In a simple 1D random walk, a particle moves left or right with equal probability. Its position after $n steps relates closely to the [[lln-proofs|Law of Large Numbers]] and the Central Limit Theorem.

### Recurrence vs Transience
Pólya's Random Walk Theorem states:
- A simple random walk on a $\mathbb{Z}^1$ or $\mathbb{Z}^2$ lattice is **recurrent** (probability of returning to the origin is 1).
- In $\mathbb{Z}^3$ or higher, it is **transient** (probability of returning is less than 1).
"A drunk man will find his way home, but a drunk bird may get lost forever."
