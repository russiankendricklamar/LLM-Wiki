Solving complex integrals requires tools beyond basic anti-derivatives.

### Integration by Parts
Derived from the product rule of differentiation:
$\int u \, dv = uv - \int v \, du$

### Partial Fractions
Used to integrate rational functions by breaking them down into simpler additive fractions, dependent on finding the roots of the denominator polynomial.

### Trigonometric Substitution
Transforms algebraic expressions containing radicals (like $\sqrt{a^2 - x^2}$) into trigonometric integrals, which are easier to solve. 
These methods are heavily utilized when calculating volumes using [[coordinate-systems|Coordinate Systems]].
