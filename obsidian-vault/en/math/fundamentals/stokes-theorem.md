## Overview
Stokes' Theorem generalizes Green's Theorem to higher dimensions, relating the surface integral of the curl of a vector field over a surface $\Sigma$ to the line integral of the vector field over its boundary $\partial \Sigma$.

## Mathematical Formulation
Let $\Sigma$ be a smooth oriented surface with boundary $\partial \Sigma$. For a vector field $\mathbf{F}$:
$$ \oint_{\partial \Sigma} \mathbf{F} \cdot d\mathbf{r} = \iint_{\Sigma} (\nabla \times \mathbf{F}) \cdot d\mathbf{S} $$

This is foundational in physics, particularly in [[maxwells-equations]].
