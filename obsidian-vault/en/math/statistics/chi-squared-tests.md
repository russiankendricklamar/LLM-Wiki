## Overview
Pearson's chi-squared ($\chi^2$) test is used to determine whether there is a statistically significant difference between expected and observed frequencies in categorical categories.

## Test Statistic
$$ \chi^2 = \sum \frac{(O_i - E_i)^2}{E_i} $$
where $O_i$ is the observed frequency and $E_i$ is the expected frequency under the null hypothesis. It is commonly used for goodness-of-fit and tests of independence.
