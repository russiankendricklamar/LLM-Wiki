# Clifford Algebras & Geometric Algebra

## Definition
Let $V$ be a vector space over $\mathbb{R}$ with a quadratic form $Q$. The Clifford algebra $\text{C}\ell(V, Q)$ is the freest unital associative algebra generated by $V$ subject to the condition:
$$ v^2 = Q(v)1 \quad \forall v \in V $$
For $u, v \in V$, this implies the anticommutator relationship:
$$ uv + vu = 2B(u, v)1 $$

## Multivectors
Elements of a Clifford algebra are called multivectors. In $\text{C}\ell_n(\mathbb{R})$, an element can be decomposed into $k$-vectors: scalars (grade 0), vectors (grade 1), bivectors (grade 2), ..., pseudoscalars (grade $n$).
The geometric product of two vectors is:
$$ ab = a \cdot b + a \wedge b $$

## Rotors and Spinors
Rotations are generated by elements called rotors, $R = \exp(-B/2)$ where $B$ is a bivector. A vector $x$ is rotated via:
$$ x' = R x R^\dagger $$
This generalizes quaternions to any dimension and provides the mathematical foundation for spinors in Dirac theory.

## Applications in Physics
In special relativity, the spacetime algebra $\text{C}\ell_{1,3}(\mathbb{R})$ simplifies Maxwell's equations to a single equation:
$$ \nabla F = J $$
where $\nabla$ is the spacetime derivative, $F$ is the Faraday bivector, and $J$ is the current.
