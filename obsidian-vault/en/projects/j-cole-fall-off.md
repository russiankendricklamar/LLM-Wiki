# J. Cole — The Fall Off Review

A **brutalist, kinetic typography** design concept built as an interactive review of J. Cole's *The Fall Off*. Inspired by high-end developer portfolios and editorial design — where the layout itself carries the weight of the argument.

## The Idea

Most music reviews live in text editors. This one lives in the browser. The ambition was to treat the album review as a design artefact: every section has its own motion language, every transition earns its place. The content and the form say the same thing.

## Stack

- **React 19** — concurrent rendering for smooth kinetic sequences
- **TypeScript** — typed component props and animation configs
- **Framer Motion** — layout animations, gesture-driven reveals, scroll-linked effects
- **Vite** — instant HMR during design iteration

## Design Language

**Brutalism** here means raw structure made visible: exposed grid, oversized type, deliberate tension between elements. No decorative gradients — only weight, spacing, and motion doing the work.

**Kinetic typography** drives the reading experience: track titles animate in on scroll, ratings reveal with spring physics, sections punctuate with hard cuts rather than fades.

## Status

Shipped as a self-contained static app. Private repo.
