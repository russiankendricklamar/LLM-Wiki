# CS01 (Credit Spread 01)

**CS01** (also known as **DVCS** or **Credit DV01**) measures the sensitivity of a financial instrument's value to a 1 basis point ($0.01\%$) change in its credit spread. It is the primary risk metric used for bond portfolios and credit derivatives like Credit Default Swaps (CDS).

## Overview & Background

Credit spread sensitivity emerged as a distinct risk measure as the credit derivatives market grew in the 1990s and 2000s. Before CDS markets developed, credit risk was managed implicitly through DV01 ([[dv01]]) — the interest rate sensitivity — since credit and rate risk were bundled together in bond yields. The development of liquid CDS markets allowed practitioners to separate the two: a bond's yield spread over swap rates is a pure credit signal, while the swap rate itself is a pure interest rate signal.

CS01 formalizes this decomposition. A corporate bond position has both rate risk (captured by DV01) and credit risk (captured by CS01). Managing both independently — hedging rate risk with interest rate swaps and credit risk with CDS — became the standard practice for investment-grade credit desks at major banks. The 2008 crisis highlighted the importance of accurate CS01 measurement, as spreads for financial names widened by thousands of basis points, dwarfing the rate risk on the same positions.

## Mathematical Framework

CS01 is the first-order derivative of instrument value $V$ with respect to credit spread $s$:

$$
\text{CS01} = -\frac{\partial V}{\partial s}
$$

Numerically, using a 1 bp bump:

$$
\text{CS01} \approx -\frac{V(s + \Delta s) - V(s)}{\Delta s}, \quad \Delta s = 0.0001 \text{ (1 bp)}
$$

The negative sign convention means CS01 is typically quoted as a **positive number representing dollar loss per 1 bp spread widening**.

### Bond CS01 via Duration

For a fixed-rate bond with price $P$, the credit spread enters through the discount rate. Approximating:

$$
\text{CS01}_{bond} \approx P \cdot D_{mod} \cdot 0.0001
$$

where $D_{mod}$ is modified duration. This is the same formula as DV01 — because for a bond, a 1 bp parallel shift in credit spreads is equivalent (to first order) to a 1 bp shift in yields.

### CDS CS01 (Risky PV01)

For a CDS contract with notional $N$, the CS01 is closely related to the **Risky PV01** (RPV01):

$$
\text{RPV01} = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \Delta t_i \cdot DF(t_i) \cdot S(t_i)
$$

where $DF(t_i)$ is the risk-free discount factor and $S(t_i)$ is the survival probability at coupon date $t_i$. The CDS CS01 is:

$$
\text{CS01}_{CDS} \approx N \cdot \text{RPV01} \cdot 0.0001
$$

For a standard 5-year CDS, RPV01 is approximately 4.5 (years), so CS01 on a $10M notional is roughly $4,500 per bp.

### Full Spread Curve CS01

In practice, CS01 is computed for each pillar of the credit spread curve (e.g., 1Y, 2Y, 3Y, 5Y, 7Y, 10Y) independently — a **bucketed CS01** or **key-rate CS01**:

$$
\text{CS01}(t_k) = -\frac{V(s_1, \dots, s_k + \Delta s, \dots, s_n) - V(s_1, \dots, s_n)}{\Delta s}
$$

This reveals whether the position's credit risk is concentrated at the short end (short-dated bonds/CDS) or long end (long-dated corporate bonds).

### Spread Duration

The **spread duration** $D_S$ is the unitless version of CS01:

$$
D_S = \frac{\text{CS01}}{V} \times 10000 \quad \text{(years)}
$$

For investment grade bonds, spread duration approximately equals modified duration. For distressed bonds trading at deep discounts, they diverge significantly.

## Key Properties

- **Sign**: CS01 is positive for long credit (long bonds, sold CDS protection) — you lose money when spreads widen. CS01 is negative for short credit (short bonds, bought CDS protection).
- **Convexity**: Like duration, CS01 is only a first-order approximation. The second-order term (**spread convexity** or **CS02**) becomes important for large spread moves.
- **Orthogonality to DV01**: For investment-grade bonds, DV01 (rate risk) and CS01 (credit risk) are approximately orthogonal — a 1 bp rate move does not change credit spreads and vice versa.
- **Non-linearity for distressed credit**: At high spread levels (>500 bps), the relationship between spread and price is highly non-linear; full repricing rather than duration approximation is required.

## Python Implementation

```python
import numpy as np
from scipy.optimize import brentq

def estimate_cs01_duration(market_value: float, modified_duration: float) -> float:
    """
    Approximate CS01 using modified duration.
    Suitable for investment-grade bonds away from distress.
    """
    return market_value * modified_duration * 0.0001

def bond_price(coupon: float, face: float, ytm: float, n_periods: int) -> float:
    """Price a fixed-rate bond given yield to maturity."""
    t = np.arange(1, n_periods + 1)
    cf = np.full(n_periods, coupon)
    cf[-1] += face
    return float(np.sum(cf / (1 + ytm) ** t))

def cs01_by_bump(
    coupon: float, face: float, ytm_base: float,
    credit_spread: float, n_periods: int, bump_bp: float = 1.0
) -> float:
    """
    Compute CS01 by repricing the bond after a 1 bp spread bump.
    Total yield = risk-free rate + credit spread.
    """
    bump = bump_bp / 10000
    price_base = bond_price(coupon, face, ytm_base + credit_spread, n_periods)
    price_bumped = bond_price(coupon, face, ytm_base + credit_spread + bump, n_periods)
    return -(price_bumped - price_base) / bump_bp  # CS01 per bp, positive for long

def cds_risky_pv01(
    maturities: np.ndarray,
    survival_probs: np.ndarray,
    discount_factors: np.ndarray
) -> float:
    """
    Compute CDS Risky PV01 (RPV01) = sum of discounted survival-weighted annuity.
    Assumes quarterly coupon payments.
    """
    dt = np.diff(np.concatenate([[0], maturities]))
    return float(np.sum(dt * discount_factors * survival_probs))

def cds_cs01(notional: float, rpv01: float) -> float:
    """CS01 of a CDS position = notional × RPV01 × 0.0001."""
    return notional * rpv01 * 0.0001

def bucketed_cs01(
    bond_cash_flows: np.ndarray,
    payment_times: np.ndarray,
    base_spreads: np.ndarray,
    risk_free_rates: np.ndarray,
    bump_pillars: list
) -> dict:
    """
    Compute bucketed CS01 — spread sensitivity at each maturity pillar.
    Bumps each pillar by 1bp independently.
    """
    def price_from_spreads(spreads):
        yields = risk_free_rates + spreads
        dfs = np.exp(-yields * payment_times)
        return float(np.dot(bond_cash_flows, dfs))

    base_price = price_from_spreads(base_spreads)
    results = {}
    for pillar_idx in bump_pillars:
        bumped = base_spreads.copy()
        bumped[pillar_idx] += 0.0001
        bumped_price = price_from_spreads(bumped)
        results[f"CS01_pillar_{pillar_idx}"] = -(bumped_price - base_price)

    return results

# Example portfolio CS01
portfolio = [
    {'ticker': 'AAPL 4.5% 2028', 'notional': 5e6, 'duration': 4.2, 'price': 102.5},
    {'ticker': 'MS 3.75% 2025', 'notional': 3e6, 'duration': 1.8, 'price': 99.1},
    {'ticker': 'BAC CDS 5Y', 'notional': -10e6, 'rpv01': 4.6, 'price': None},
]

cs01_values = {}
for pos in portfolio:
    if pos['price'] is not None:
        mv = pos['notional'] * pos['price'] / 100
        cs01_values[pos['ticker']] = estimate_cs01_duration(mv, pos['duration'])
    else:
        cs01_values[pos['ticker']] = cds_cs01(pos['notional'], pos['rpv01'])

total_cs01 = sum(cs01_values.values())
```

## Parameter Table

| Symbol | Name | Description | Typical Range |
|:---|:---:|:---|:---|
| $s$ | Credit spread | OAS or CDS spread over risk-free | 10 – 2000 bps |
| $\Delta s$ | Spread bump | Standard CS01 shift | 1 bp = 0.0001 |
| $D_{mod}$ | Modified duration | Interest rate / spread duration | 0.5 – 15 years |
| $\text{RPV01}$ | Risky PV01 | Discounted survival-weighted annuity | 0.5 – 5 (years) |
| $N$ | Notional | Face value of position | Any |
| $D_S$ | Spread duration | CS01 / market value × 10,000 | ≈ $D_{mod}$ for IG |

## Limitations & Extensions

**Limitations:**
- **Duration approximation breaks down**: For distressed issuers (spreads > 500 bps), duration-based CS01 significantly overestimates true sensitivity; full repricing is mandatory.
- **Curve shape assumption**: Parallel spread shift assumes the entire credit curve moves by 1 bp uniformly. Real spread moves are rarely parallel — bucketed CS01 is more informative.
- **Recovery rate sensitivity**: CS01 from CDS pricing depends on the assumed recovery rate $R$; a 10% change in $R$ can move CS01 by 5–10%.
- **Basis risk**: The spread of a bond over swaps (asset swap spread) may not move 1-for-1 with the CDS spread (CDS-bond basis), so CDS CS01 may not perfectly hedge bond CS01.

**Extensions:**
- **CS02 (spread convexity)**: Second derivative of price with respect to spread; important for large spread moves and structured products.
- **Scenario CS01**: Compute CS01 conditional on specific macro scenarios (e.g., recession spreads widening 200 bps on average).
- **Factor CS01**: Decompose CS01 into systematic (sector, rating) and idiosyncratic components using a credit factor model.

## Practical Applications

1. **Hedging**: Opening CDS protection positions to zero out net CS01 on a bond portfolio — achieving a spread-neutral book.
2. **Risk Limits**: Setting maximum allowed CS01 exposure per issuer (e.g., $50,000/bp), per sector (e.g., $500,000/bp), and in aggregate.
3. **P&L Attribution**: Daily CS01 × daily spread change explains the credit component of bond P&L; residual is basis risk and convexity.
4. **Regulatory Capital**: FRTB (Fundamental Review of the Trading Book) uses sensitivities-based method (SBM) where CS01 is an input to standardized capital charges.
5. **Relative Value**: Comparing CS01-normalized yield differences between two issuers identifies cheap/rich bonds on a credit-risk-adjusted basis.
