# DV01 (Dollar Value of a 01)

**DV01** (Dollar Value of a 01) measures the price sensitivity of a fixed-income instrument to a 1 basis point ($0.01\%$) change in yield. Unlike duration, which is expressed in years or percentage terms, DV01 provides a direct monetary value of the risk, making it the standard tool for hedging and aggregating interest rate risk across a portfolio.

## Overview & Background

The concept of dollar duration — translating rate sensitivity into money terms — emerged alongside the professionalization of fixed income markets in the 1970s and 1980s. While Macaulay duration (1938) and modified duration had been the academic framework, traders needed a measure that answered a practical question: "If rates move 1 basis point, how many dollars do I make or lose?"

DV01 answered that question directly. Its widespread adoption was driven by the bond trading desks at major investment banks (Salomon Brothers, Goldman Sachs) where the risk of large bond positions had to be communicated rapidly and clearly. Today DV01 is the universal language of fixed income risk: swap desks, bond funds, and government treasuries all express rate exposure in DV01 terms.

The closely related measure **PV01** (Present Value of a Basis Point) is used interchangeably with DV01 in most contexts, with minor definitional differences in swap and annuity pricing.

## Mathematical Framework

### Definition

DV01 is the absolute dollar change in the price of a fixed-income instrument per 1 basis point change in yield:

$$
\text{DV01} = -\frac{\partial P}{\partial y} \times 0.0001
$$

Numerically (finite difference):

$$
\text{DV01} \approx -\frac{P(y + \Delta y) - P(y)}{\Delta y / 0.0001}, \quad \Delta y = 0.0001
$$

The negative sign ensures DV01 is positive for a long bond position (prices fall when yields rise).

### Relationship to Modified Duration

DV01 is directly proportional to modified duration $D_{mod}$:

$$
\text{DV01} = P \times D_{mod} \times 0.0001
$$

where $P$ is the **full price** (dirty price including accrued interest) and $D_{mod}$ is:

$$
D_{mod} = \frac{D_{Mac}}{1 + y/m}
$$

with $D_{Mac}$ the Macaulay duration and $m$ the number of coupon payments per year.

### Bond Pricing and Yield

For a bond with $n$ coupon payments of $c$ and face value $F$:

$$
P(y) = \sum_{i=1}^n \frac{c}{(1 + y/m)^i} + \frac{F}{(1 + y/m)^n}
$$

The analytical derivative:

$$
\frac{\partial P}{\partial y} = -\frac{1}{m} \sum_{i=1}^n \frac{i \cdot c}{(1 + y/m)^{i+1}} - \frac{n \cdot F}{m(1 + y/m)^{n+1}}
$$

So $\text{DV01} = -\frac{\partial P}{\partial y} \times 0.0001$ is exact for bullet bonds.

### Convexity Correction

For large yield moves, the first-order DV01 approximation is insufficient. The second-order term involves **convexity** $\mathcal{C}$:

$$
\Delta P \approx -\text{DV01} \times \Delta y \times 10000 + \frac{1}{2} P \mathcal{C} (\Delta y)^2
$$

where $\mathcal{C} = \frac{1}{P} \frac{\partial^2 P}{\partial y^2}$. For a parallel shift $\Delta y$ in basis points:

$$
\Delta P \approx -\text{DV01} \times \Delta y_{bp} + \frac{1}{2} P \mathcal{C} \times (\Delta y_{bp} \times 0.0001)^2
$$

Positive convexity (standard bonds) means prices fall less than DV01 predicts when rates rise, and rise more than DV01 predicts when rates fall — a desirable property.

### Interest Rate Swap DV01

For a **receive-fixed swap** (notional $N$, fixed rate $K$, floating LIBOR/SOFR):

$$
V_{swap} = \text{PV(Fixed leg)} - \text{PV(Floating leg)}
$$

$$
\text{DV01}_{swap} \approx N \times \text{annuity factor} \times 0.0001
$$

where the annuity factor $= \sum_i \Delta t_i \cdot DF_i$ is the sum of discounted period lengths. For a 5-year swap, DV01 ≈ 4.5 per 1 million notional (i.e., $450 per $1M notional per bp).

### Yield Curve Key-Rate DV01

Rather than a single parallel DV01, **key-rate DV01** (KR-DV01) measures sensitivity to each maturity bucket independently:

$$
\text{KR-DV01}(t_k) = -\frac{P(\text{rate}(t_k) + \Delta y, \text{others fixed}) - P}{\Delta y / 0.0001}
$$

A 10-year bond's KR-DV01 is concentrated at 10Y; a swap ladder has KR-DV01 at multiple tenors.

## Key Properties

- **Additivity**: DV01 is additive across positions — total portfolio DV01 is the sum of individual position DV01s.
- **Sign convention**: Long fixed income → positive DV01 (lose money when rates rise). Short → negative DV01.
- **Units**: Dollars per basis point. A DV01 of $10,000 means a 1 bp rate increase costs $10,000.
- **Scaling**: DV01 scales linearly with notional. Doubling position size doubles DV01.
- **Tenor dependence**: Longer maturity bonds have higher DV01 per dollar of notional.

## Python Implementation

```python
import numpy as np
import pandas as pd
from scipy.optimize import brentq

def bond_price(coupon_rate: float, face: float, ytm: float,
               n_periods: int, freq: int = 2) -> float:
    """Price a fixed-rate bond."""
    c = coupon_rate * face / freq
    t = np.arange(1, n_periods + 1)
    cf = np.full(n_periods, c)
    cf[-1] += face
    return float(np.sum(cf / (1 + ytm / freq) ** t))

def calculate_dv01(
    coupon_rate: float, face: float, ytm: float,
    n_periods: int, freq: int = 2, bump_bp: float = 1.0
) -> dict:
    """
    Compute DV01 by finite difference and via modified duration.

    Returns DV01 (bump), DV01 (duration), modified duration, convexity.
    """
    bump = bump_bp / 10000
    p0 = bond_price(coupon_rate, face, ytm, n_periods, freq)
    p_up = bond_price(coupon_rate, face, ytm + bump, n_periods, freq)
    p_dn = bond_price(coupon_rate, face, ytm - bump, n_periods, freq)

    dv01_bump = -(p_up - p0) / bump_bp
    d_mod = (p_dn - p_up) / (2 * bump * p0)  # Modified duration
    dv01_dur = p0 * d_mod * 0.0001
    convexity = (p_up - 2 * p0 + p_dn) / (p0 * bump ** 2)

    return {
        'price': p0,
        'dv01_bump': dv01_bump,
        'dv01_duration': dv01_dur,
        'modified_duration': d_mod,
        'convexity': convexity
    }

def portfolio_dv01(
    positions: list[dict]
) -> dict:
    """
    Aggregate DV01 across a portfolio of fixed-income positions.

    Each position dict: {'coupon': float, 'ytm': float, 'n_periods': int,
                         'notional': float, 'freq': int}
    """
    total_dv01 = 0.0
    breakdown = []
    for pos in positions:
        result = calculate_dv01(pos['coupon'], pos['notional'],
                                pos['ytm'], pos['n_periods'],
                                pos.get('freq', 2))
        # Scale for notional (bond_price uses face=notional)
        breakdown.append({
            'description': pos.get('name', ''),
            'notional': pos['notional'],
            'dv01': result['dv01_bump'],
            'duration': result['modified_duration']
        })
        total_dv01 += result['dv01_bump']

    return {'total_dv01': total_dv01, 'breakdown': breakdown}

def dv01_neutral_hedge_ratio(
    position_dv01: float, hedge_dv01_per_unit: float
) -> float:
    """
    Compute the number of hedge units needed for a DV01-neutral position.
    hedge_dv01_per_unit: DV01 of 1 unit of the hedge instrument (e.g., 1 futures contract)
    """
    return -position_dv01 / hedge_dv01_per_unit

def yield_to_maturity(
    price: float, coupon_rate: float, face: float,
    n_periods: int, freq: int = 2
) -> float:
    """Solve for YTM given market price using Brent's method."""
    def objective(ytm):
        return bond_price(coupon_rate, face, ytm, n_periods, freq) - price
    return brentq(objective, 0.0001, 0.99)

# Example: US Treasury-like portfolio
portfolio = [
    {'name': '2Y Note', 'coupon': 0.045, 'ytm': 0.046, 'n_periods': 4,
     'notional': 1_000_000, 'freq': 2},
    {'name': '10Y Bond', 'coupon': 0.042, 'ytm': 0.044, 'n_periods': 20,
     'notional': 500_000, 'freq': 2},
    {'name': '30Y Bond', 'coupon': 0.040, 'ytm': 0.043, 'n_periods': 60,
     'notional': 250_000, 'freq': 2},
]

agg = portfolio_dv01(portfolio)
note_dv01 = calculate_dv01(0.045, 1_000_000, 0.046, 4)
hedge_ratio = dv01_neutral_hedge_ratio(agg['total_dv01'], hedge_dv01_per_unit=63.0)
```

## Parameter Table

|:---|:---:|:---|:---|
| $P$ | Full price | Dirty price including accrued interest | 70 – 130 per 100 face |
| $D_{mod}$ | Modified duration | DV01 / (price × 0.0001) | 0.5 – 20 years |
| $\mathcal{C}$ | Convexity | Second-order rate sensitivity | 10 – 500 |
| $\Delta y$ | Yield bump | Standard DV01 shift | 1 bp = 0.0001 |
| $m$ | Coupon frequency | Payments per year | 1, 2, 4, 12 |
| $n$ | Periods | Total coupon periods | 2 – 120 |

## Limitations & Extensions

**Limitations:**
- **Parallel shift only**: DV01 measures sensitivity to a uniform 1 bp shift across all maturities. Non-parallel moves (steepening, flattening, butterfly) require key-rate DV01 analysis.
- **First-order only**: Convexity is ignored; for large moves (> 50 bps) or callable bonds, convexity correction is material.
- **Callable/putable bonds**: Embedded options make duration and DV01 path-dependent; effective duration from option-adjusted spread (OAS) models replaces modified duration.
- **Credit-contaminated yield**: Bond YTM contains both rate and credit components; using YTM-based DV01 conflates rate and credit risk. OAS-based DV01 isolates pure rate sensitivity.

**Extensions:**
- **Key-Rate DV01**: Sensitivity to each point on the yield curve independently — the standard tool for curve trading (steepeners, flatteners, butterflies).
- **OAS DV01**: For mortgage-backed securities (MBS) and callable bonds, use option-adjusted spread to compute pure rate sensitivity.
- **[[cs01]]**: The credit-spread analog of DV01 — sensitivity to a 1 bp shift in credit spreads.
- **Futures DV01**: Treasury futures have a "cheapest-to-deliver" adjusted DV01; widely used for DV01-neutral hedging of bond portfolios.

## Practical Applications

1. **Hedging**: Match DV01 of a bond position with an opposite DV01 in Treasury futures or interest rate swaps for a rate-neutral book.
2. **Risk Aggregation**: Sum DV01 across all fixed-income positions in a portfolio to understand total exposure to a parallel rate shift.
3. **Curve Trading**: Monitor KR-DV01 at 2Y, 5Y, 10Y, 30Y to position for flattening/steepening trades while remaining DV01-neutral overall.
4. **FRTB Capital**: Under the Fundamental Review of the Trading Book, DV01-based sensitivities feed the standardized approach (SA) interest rate risk capital calculation.
5. **Asset-Liability Management**: Banks compute the DV01 of their loan and deposit books to manage Net Interest Margin (NIM) sensitivity to rate changes.
