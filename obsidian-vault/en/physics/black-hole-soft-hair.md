# Black Hole Soft Hair

## Theoretical Foundation
The "No-Hair Theorem" of classical general relativity states that black holes are characterized only by their mass, charge, and angular momentum. However, Hawking, Perry, and Strominger (HPS) proposed that black holes possess "soft hair"—quantum excitations of zero energy. This hair arises from the infinite number of conserved charges associated with [[asymptotic-symmetries]] (BMS supertranslations and superrotations) that act on the black hole horizon, suggesting that black holes can store far more information than previously thought.

## Mathematical Formulation
The soft hair is described by the action of large gauge transformations on the black hole metric. For a supertranslation $f(z, \bar{z})$, the corresponding charge $Q_f$ is:
$$ Q_f = \frac{1}{4\pi G} \int_{\mathcal{H}} f m_{AB} \sigma^{AB} $$
where $\mathcal{H}$ is the horizon and $m_{AB}$ is related to the gravitational data. These charges commute with the Hamiltonian, meaning they don't change the energy of the black hole but do change its microstate.

## Physical Significance & Information Theory
Soft hair provides a potential resolution to the Black Hole Information Paradox. If a black hole has an infinite number of soft charges, the information of the infalling matter could be encoded in the soft hair on the horizon. When the black hole evaporates via Hawking radiation, these soft charges could imprint the information onto the outgoing "soft" photons and gravitons, preserving unitarity. This shifts the focus from the singularity to the infrared structure of the gravitational field at the horizon.
