# Black Hole Soft Hair

## Prerequisites

[[soft-theorems]], [[bms-group]]

## Theoretical Foundation
The classical "No-Hair Theorem" states that stationary black holes in general relativity are completely characterized by just three parameters: mass $M$, electric charge $Q$, and angular momentum $J$. All other information about the matter that formed the black hole is supposedly lost. This poses a profound problem: if a black hole evaporates via Hawking radiation, the outgoing radiation appears thermal and carries no information about the initial state, apparently violating quantum mechanical unitarity.

In 2016, Hawking, Perry, and Strominger (HPS) proposed a resolution through soft hair — zero-energy quantum excitations of the black hole horizon generated by BMS supertranslations and electromagnetic large gauge transformations. The key insight is that supertranslations act non-trivially on the horizon, just as they do at null infinity, creating an infinite number of distinct microstates that all have the same mass, charge, and angular momentum but differ in their soft hair configuration. These soft degrees of freedom can in principle encode the information of infalling matter.

The proposal connects three previously separate developments: the BMS asymptotic symmetry group, the Hawking-Perry-Strominger charge algebra on the horizon, and the Pasterski triangle relating soft theorems, memory effects, and asymptotic symmetries. Soft hair does not fully resolve the information paradox — it does not specify how information is decoded from Hawking radiation — but it establishes that black holes have far more internal structure than the no-hair theorem suggests.

## Mathematical Formulation
The supertranslation charge on the future horizon $\mathcal{H}^+$ is:
$$ Q_f^{\mathcal{H}} = \frac{1}{4\pi G}\int_{\mathcal{H}^+} d^2z\,\gamma_{z\bar{z}}\,f(z,\bar{z})\,T_{uu} $$
where $T_{uu}$ is the energy flux through the horizon and $f(z,\bar{z})$ is the supertranslation parameter. The charge satisfies $[Q_f^{\mathcal{H}}, H] = 0$, meaning soft hair excitations are genuinely zero-energy and do not affect the black hole mass.

The soft hair state is created by acting with a supertranslation on a Schwarzschild solution:
$$ |\text{BH with hair}\rangle = e^{i Q_f} |\text{Schwarzschild}\rangle $$
The resulting spacetime has the same ADM mass but a modified metric near the horizon:
$$ C_{zz}^{\mathcal{H}} \to C_{zz}^{\mathcal{H}} + 2D_z^2 f $$
where $D_z^2 f$ is the second covariant derivative of $f$ on the sphere.

Matching conditions between $\mathcal{I}^-_+$ (late past null infinity) and $\mathcal{H}^+$ (future horizon) imply that soft hair on the horizon is in one-to-one correspondence with the soft photon and graviton charges measured at null infinity:
$$ Q_f^{\mathcal{I}^-_+} = Q_f^{\mathcal{H}^+} $$
This is the key statement: information about infalling matter imprints on the horizon as soft hair, which is then related to measurable soft charges at null infinity.

## Key Properties
- **Zero energy**: Soft hair excitations carry exactly zero energy, so an infinite number of them can be added without changing the black hole mass.
- **Infinite degeneracy**: The number of distinct soft hair configurations is infinite (parametrized by functions on $S^2$), providing the microstate counting needed for a finite Bekenstein-Hawking entropy.
- **Horizon vs. infinity**: The soft charges at the horizon match antipodally to soft charges at past null infinity — the same information that entered the black hole is in principle accessible at $\mathcal{I}^-$.
- **Electromagnetic hair**: Analogous soft hair exists for the electromagnetic field via large $U(1)$ gauge transformations; every charged black hole carries soft photon hair.
- **Limitations**: Soft hair alone is insufficient to fully decode all infalling information — the mechanism for transferring Planck-scale information into soft modes remains an open problem.

## Physical Interpretation
The physical picture is that every time a particle falls into a black hole, it excites a supertranslation mode of the horizon — a soft graviton imprinted on the event horizon geometry. The horizon, far from being featureless, is covered in an intricate pattern of soft excitations that collectively encode the history of everything that fell in.

When the black hole radiates via Hawking radiation, the outgoing quanta carry soft charges that in principle encode this information. The challenge — not yet fully solved — is showing that this soft encoding is sufficient to reconstruct the full quantum state of the infalling matter from the Hawking radiation.

## Connections to Information Theory & Holography
Soft hair provides a concrete realization of the holographic principle: information about a 3D process (matter falling into a 3D black hole) is encoded on a 2D surface (the horizon). Each soft hair mode corresponds to a degree of freedom of the holographic screen.

In the [[celestial-holography]] framework, the matching of horizon soft charges to null infinity charges is the flat-space analog of the holographic dictionary: bulk operators near the horizon are dual to boundary operators at $\mathcal{I}$. The infinite-dimensional BMS symmetry plays the role of the conformal symmetry in AdS/CFT.
