# Gravitational Waves & General Relativity (GR)

## Einstein Field Equations
General Relativity describes gravity as the curvature of spacetime. The Einstein field equations relate the metric $g_{\mu\nu}$ to the energy-momentum tensor $T_{\mu\nu}$:
$$ R_{\mu\nu} - \frac{1}{2}R g_{\mu\nu} = 8\pi G T_{\mu\nu} $$
where $R_{\mu\nu}$ is the Ricci curvature tensor and $R$ is the Ricci scalar.

## Linearized Gravity
Gravitational waves are small perturbations $h_{\mu\nu}$ of the flat Minkowski metric $\eta_{\mu\nu}$:
$$ g_{\mu\nu} = \eta_{\mu\nu} + h_{\mu\nu}, \quad |h_{\mu\nu}| \ll 1 $$
In the transverse-traceless (TT) gauge, the linearized vacuum equations reduce to a wave equation:
$$ \square h_{\mu\nu} = 0 $$

## Polarizations
Gravitational waves have two independent polarization modes:
* **Plus polarization ($h_+$):** Stretches space along one axis and squeezes it along the orthogonal axis.
* **Cross polarization ($h_\times$):** Same effect but rotated by 45 degrees.

## Detections and LIGO
The first direct detection of gravitational waves by LIGO (GW150914) from a binary black hole merger confirmed a key prediction of Einstein's theory. These waves carry information about strong-field gravity and provide a new way to observe the universe.
