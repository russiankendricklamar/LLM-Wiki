# Gravitational Waves & General Relativity (GR)

## Theoretical Foundation
General Relativity (GR), formulated by Einstein in 1915, describes gravity as the curvature of a four-dimensional spacetime manifold. Matter and energy curve spacetime; curved spacetime tells matter how to move. This geometric interpretation, replacing Newton's instantaneous action-at-a-distance force with a local field theory, leads to a rich set of predictions absent from Newtonian gravity: light bending, gravitational redshift, black holes, and gravitational waves.

Gravitational waves are ripples in the fabric of spacetime, propagating at the speed of light, generated by accelerating mass distributions with non-zero time-varying quadrupole moment. Einstein predicted them in 1916 directly from linearized GR, but doubted their physical reality for decades. Indirect evidence came from the Hulse-Taylor binary pulsar (1974), which loses energy at a rate precisely matching GR's prediction for gravitational wave emission. Direct detection by LIGO in 2015 (announced 2016) confirmed GR in the strong-field, highly-dynamical regime for the first time.

Beyond their direct observational significance, gravitational waves are the arena for the infrared structure of gravity: soft gravitons, BMS symmetries, gravitational memory, and the [[pasterski-triangle]] all concern the behavior of gravitational radiation in the limit of zero frequency or large distance.

## Mathematical Formulation
The Einstein field equations relate spacetime curvature to the energy-momentum content:
$$ G_{\mu\nu} \equiv R_{\mu\nu} - \frac{1}{2}R\,g_{\mu\nu} = 8\pi G\,T_{\mu\nu} $$
where $R_{\mu\nu}$ is the Ricci tensor, $R = g^{\mu\nu}R_{\mu\nu}$ is the Ricci scalar, and $G_{\mu\nu}$ is the Einstein tensor.

For weak fields, writing $g_{\mu\nu} = \eta_{\mu\nu} + h_{\mu\nu}$ with $|h_{\mu\nu}| \ll 1$ and choosing the Lorenz gauge $\partial^\mu \bar{h}_{\mu\nu} = 0$ (where $\bar{h}_{\mu\nu} = h_{\mu\nu} - \frac{1}{2}\eta_{\mu\nu}h$), the linearized vacuum equations reduce to:
$$ \Box \bar{h}_{\mu\nu} = 0 $$
Solutions are plane waves $h_{\mu\nu} = \epsilon_{\mu\nu} e^{ik_\alpha x^\alpha} + \text{c.c.}$ with $k^\mu k_\mu = 0$ (null wavevector). In the transverse-traceless (TT) gauge, only two independent polarization modes survive:
$$ h_{\mu\nu}^{\rm TT} = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & h_+ & h_\times & 0 \\ 0 & h_\times & -h_+ & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{pmatrix} $$
The leading-order power radiated by a non-relativistic source is given by the quadrupole formula:
$$ P = \frac{G}{5c^5} \left\langle \dddot{Q}_{ij}\dddot{Q}^{ij} \right\rangle $$
where $Q_{ij} = \int \rho(x_i x_j - \frac{1}{3}\delta_{ij}r^2)\,d^3x$ is the traceless quadrupole moment tensor.

## Key Properties
- **Two polarizations**: Gravitational waves have $+$ and $\times$ polarizations (tensor, spin-2), contrasting with the two vector polarizations of electromagnetic waves. This helicity-2 nature reflects the spin-2 graviton.
- **Quadrupole emission**: Unlike electromagnetism (dipole emission), the lowest multipole for GW emission is the quadrupole; monopole and dipole are forbidden by conservation of energy-momentum.
- **Strain sensitivity**: LIGO detects strains $h \sim 10^{-21}$, meaning a change in arm length of $\sim 10^{-18}$ m — one-thousandth the diameter of a proton — over a 4 km baseline.
- **Speed**: Gravitational waves propagate at exactly $c$; the multi-messenger observation GW170817 + GRB 170817A confirmed $|v_{GW} - c|/c < 10^{-15}$.
- **Peeling**: Near $\mathcal{I}^+$, the Weyl tensor components $\Psi_0,\ldots,\Psi_4$ peel off at different rates in $r^{-1}$; the leading radiation is $\Psi_4 \sim r^{-1}$ and encodes the outgoing gravitational wave strain.

## Physical Interpretation
A gravitational wave stretches and squeezes space transversely to its direction of propagation. For the $+$ polarization, a ring of test masses is distorted into an ellipse along the $x$-axis, then the $y$-axis, alternating at the wave frequency. For the $\times$ polarization, the same happens but rotated by $45°$.

The LIGO detection GW150914 corresponds to two black holes of $\sim 36 M_\odot$ and $\sim 29 M_\odot$ merging at $\sim 410$ Mpc, releasing $\sim 3 M_\odot c^2$ of energy in $\sim 0.2$ s — briefly outshining all stars in the observable universe in gravitational wave luminosity. The chirp waveform (increasing frequency and amplitude) matches GR predictions to sub-percent accuracy.

## Connections to Information Theory & Holography
Gravitational waves carry information about strong-field gravity directly to null infinity $\mathcal{I}^+$. The Bondi news tensor $N_{zz} = \partial_u C_{zz}$ encodes all outgoing radiation, and the soft (zero-frequency) part of this news is directly related to the BMS soft charges and [[gravitational-memory]] effects.

In [[celestial-holography]], each graviton insertion in a scattering amplitude corresponds to a conformal primary operator on the celestial sphere. The collinear limits of graviton amplitudes produce the celestial OPE, and the soft limits reproduce the Ward identities of the BMS and $w_{1+\infty}$ symmetries — making gravitational waves not just astrophysical signals but windows into the holographic structure of quantum gravity.
