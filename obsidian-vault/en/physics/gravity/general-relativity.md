# General Relativity (GR): The Geometry of Spacetime

General Relativity, published by **Albert Einstein** in 1915, is the modern theory of gravitation. It replaced the Newtonian concept of "force" with the concept of **Spacetime Curvature**. In GR, mass and energy tell spacetime how to curve, and curved spacetime tells matter how to move.

## 1. The Einstein Field Equations (EFE)

The heart of the theory is a set of 10 coupled non-linear partial differential equations:
$$G_{\mu\nu} + \Lambda g_{\mu\nu} = \frac{8\pi G}{c^4} T_{\mu\nu}$$
Where:
- **$G_{\mu\nu}$ (Einstein Tensor)**: Describes the local curvature of spacetime.
- **$g_{\mu\nu}$ (Metric Tensor)**: The "ruler" of the universe, defining distances and angles.
- **$T_{\mu\nu}$ (Stress-Energy Tensor)**: Describes the density and flow of energy and momentum (the source of gravity).
- **$\Lambda$ (Cosmological Constant)**: Represents the energy density of empty space (Dark Energy).

## 2. The Equivalence Principle

Einstein's "happiest thought" was that a person in a falling elevator feels weightless.
- **Weak Equivalence Principle**: Gravitational mass is exactly equal to inertial mass.
- **Consequence**: Gravity is not an external force but a property of the coordinate system. You cannot distinguish between uniform acceleration and a uniform gravitational field.

## 3. Key Solutions and Phenomena

### A. Schwarzschild Metric
The first exact solution to EFE, describing the spacetime around a non-rotating, spherical mass (like a star or a black hole). It predicts the **Event Horizon**—the point of no return.

### B. Time Dilation
Gravity slows down time. A clock on a massive planet ticks slower than a clock in deep space. This is a real effect that must be corrected for in **GPS satellites**.

### C. Gravitational Lensing
Light follows [[manifold|Geodesics]] in curved space. When light passes near a massive galaxy, its path is bent, magnifying and distorting the image of objects behind it. This is our primary tool for mapping **Dark Matter**.

## 4. Modern Frontier: Numerical Relativity and AI

- **Black Hole Mergers**: Solving EFE for two colliding black holes is so complex it requires supercomputers.
- **AI for GR**: Physicists now use **PINNs** (Physics-Informed Neural Networks) to solve these equations 100x faster, allowing for real-time analysis of **Gravitational Waves**.
