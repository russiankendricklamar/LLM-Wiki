# Gravitational Entropy and Holography

Entropy in gravity is strange. Unlike in ordinary thermodynamics — where entropy scales with volume — the maximum entropy of any gravitational system scales with the **area** of its boundary. This is not an approximation; it is a structural feature of theories with gravity, first forced on us by black hole thermodynamics and then elevated to the **holographic principle**. The chain of ideas running from Bekenstein to 't Hooft to Ryu-Takayanagi to the island formula is one of the deepest threads in modern theoretical physics.

## The Bekenstein Bound

Bekenstein (1981) derived an upper bound on the entropy of any matter system contained within a sphere of radius $R$ and total energy $E$:

$$
S \le \frac{2\pi\, k_B\, R\, E}{\hbar\, c}.
$$

The bound follows from a gedanken experiment: if $S$ exceeds the right-hand side, one can form a black hole smaller than the system, whose Bekenstein-Hawking entropy is less than $S$, violating the generalised second law.

For a black hole itself, $E = Mc^2$ and $R = 2GM/c^2$, so the bound is saturated: $S_\text{BH} = 2\pi k_B \cdot 2GM/c^2 \cdot Mc^2 / (\hbar c) = 4\pi G M^2 k_B / \hbar c$, matching the Bekenstein-Hawking formula exactly.

## The Holographic Principle

't Hooft (1993) and Susskind (1995) drew the radical conclusion: the degrees of freedom of any region of space are **not** proportional to its volume but to the area of its boundary:

$$
N_\text{dof} \le \frac{A}{4\,\ell_P^2}.
$$

A local quantum field theory has $\exp(V/\ell_P^3)$ states per unit volume — exponentially more than the holographic bound $\exp(A/4\ell_P^2)$. When gravity is included, most of those states do not exist: they would require so much energy that a black hole would form, and the black hole entropy is only $A/4$. The true Hilbert space of quantum gravity is far smaller than local QFT would suggest.

This is the holographic principle: a theory of quantum gravity in $d+1$ dimensions is equivalently described by a quantum theory (without gravity) in $d$ dimensions on the boundary.

## Covariant Entropy Bound (Bousso)

The holographic principle, as stated, is ambiguous: which area bounds which region? Bousso (1999) gave a covariant formulation. For any spatial surface $B$ with area $A(B)$, define a **light sheet** $L(B)$ — a null hypersurface generated by light rays from $B$ along which the expansion is non-positive. The **Bousso bound**:

$$
S(L) \le \frac{A(B)}{4\,G\,\hbar/c^3},
$$

where $S(L)$ is the entropy of matter passing through $L$. This is Lorentz-covariant and holds in cosmological spacetimes, not just for static black holes. It encompasses both the Bekenstein bound and the Bekenstein-Hawking entropy as special cases.

## Ryu-Takayanagi Formula

In [[ads-cft]], entanglement entropy of a boundary region $A$ is given by the area of a minimal bulk surface:

$$
S_A^\text{RT} = \frac{\text{Area}(\gamma_A)}{4\,G_N}.
$$

This is the precise implementation of holography: geometric area in the bulk computes quantum information on the boundary. The generalisation (Faulkner-Lewkowycz-Maldacena, 2013) includes bulk quantum corrections:

$$
S_A = \frac{\text{Area}(\gamma_A)}{4\,G_N} + S_\text{bulk}(\Sigma_A),
$$

where $\Sigma_A$ is the bulk region bounded by $\gamma_A \cup A$. This is the **quantum extremal surface** (QES) formula: extremise over the location of the surface the combination $\text{Area}/4G_N + S_\text{bulk}$.

## The Page Curve

For an evaporating black hole, the entanglement entropy $S_\text{rad}(t)$ of the Hawking radiation should — if information is preserved — follow the **Page curve**: rise to a maximum at the Page time $t_\text{Page} \approx t_\text{evap}/2$, then fall back to zero as the black hole finishes evaporating.

Semi-classical Hawking theory predicts $S_\text{rad}$ rises monotonically — the information paradox. The island formula (see below) reproduces the Page curve from a gravitational calculation.

## The Island Formula

Penington (2019) and Almheiri-Engelhardt-Marolf-Maxfield (2019) derived a formula for the entropy of Hawking radiation that reproduces the Page curve:

$$
S_\text{rad} = \min_I\!\left[\,\text{ext}_I\!\left(\frac{A(\partial I)}{4\,G_N} + S_\text{matter}(\text{rad}\cup I)\right)\right].
$$

Here $I$ is an **island** — a region inside the black hole. The entropy is computed by extremising over all possible islands and taking the minimum:

- **Early times**: the minimum is at $I = \emptyset$, giving the rising semi-classical entropy.
- **After the Page time**: a non-trivial island $I$ appears inside the black hole, and the entropy decreases. The island's boundary $\partial I$ is a quantum extremal surface.

The island formula emerges from the gravitational path integral via **replica wormholes**: computing $\text{tr}\,\rho^n$ using Euclidean gravity finds saddle points where replica copies of the black hole geometry are connected by wormholes. In the limit $n\to 1$, these reproduce the island formula. The calculation is done within semi-classical gravity — no complete quantum gravity theory needed.

## Quantum Extremal Surfaces and Emergent Spacetime

The quantum extremal surface prescription says: the entropy is not simply the area of the minimal surface, but the minimum over all surfaces of $\text{Area}/4G_N + S_\text{bulk}$. At late times in black hole evaporation, the QES jumps from the empty configuration to a surface inside the black hole, encoding black hole interior degrees of freedom in the radiation's Hilbert space.

This is the concrete realisation of **emergent spacetime from entanglement**: the black hole interior is not causally accessible to outside observers, but its quantum information is encoded in the entanglement structure of the radiation. The ER=EPR conjecture (Maldacena-Susskind, 2013) suggests that entangled particles are connected by Einstein-Rosen bridges — wormholes — and replica wormholes are the Euclidean version of this.

## The Generalised Second Law

Incorporating quantum corrections, the generalised second law states

$$
\frac{d}{dt}\left[\frac{A}{4\,G_N} + S_\text{outside matter}\right] \ge 0,
$$

a combination of gravitational and matter entropy that never decreases. This holds even as the black hole evaporates ($A$ decreases) because the radiated entropy increases at least as fast. The island formula is consistent with the GSL by construction: the entropy computed always equals the minimum of the generalised entropy functional, which is non-decreasing.

## Holography Beyond AdS

The holographic principle is not restricted to anti-de Sitter space. The **Bousso-Engelhardt** quantum focusing conjecture (2015) extends the QES prescription to arbitrary spacetimes. Cosmological holography (dS/CFT) remains poorly understood. Flat-space holography — relevant for the real world — is the subject of [[celestial-holography]], where scattering amplitudes in 4d Minkowski map to correlators of a 2d CFT on the celestial sphere.

In all these contexts, the entropy-area relationship is the anchor: wherever holography holds, it is because the entropy of gravitational systems is bounded by area, not volume, and the area is computed by an extremal surface procedure generalising $S = A/4G_N$.
