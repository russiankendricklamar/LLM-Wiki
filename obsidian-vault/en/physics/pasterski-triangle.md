# Pasterski's Triangle Theory

## Theoretical Foundation
Pasterski's Triangle is a profound synthesis in theoretical physics that establishes an equivalence between three seemingly unrelated concepts in gauge theories and gravity: Soft Theorems, Asymptotic Symmetries, and Memory Effects. Developed largely by Sabrina Pasterski and colleagues, this framework shows that the "soft" limit of scattering amplitudes (IR physics) is dictated by the symmetry structure of spacetime at infinity (UV physics), which in turn produces measurable physical changes (Memory).

## Mathematical Formulation
The triangle represents the following three identities:
1. **Soft Theorems $\leftrightarrow$ Asymptotic Symmetries**: Weinberg's Soft Graviton Theorem is the Ward identity for [[bms-group]] supertranslations.
2. **Asymptotic Symmetries $\leftrightarrow$ Memory Effects**: Large gauge transformations (supertranslations) shift the vacuum, resulting in the [[gravitational-memory]] effect.
3. **Memory Effects $\leftrightarrow$ Soft Theorems**: The Fourier transform of the memory effect is the soft limit of the radiated field.

## Physical Significance & Holography
Pasterski's Triangle provides a roadmap for [[celestial-holography]]. It suggests that the S-matrix of quantum gravity in 4D Minkowski space can be mapped to a 2D Conformal Field Theory (CFT) on the "celestial sphere" at null infinity. This discovery has major implications for the black hole information paradox, as it suggests that infrared divergences and soft particles are not just nuisances, but crucial carriers of information that ensure the conservation of charges at the boundary of spacetime.
