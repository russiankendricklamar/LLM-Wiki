# BMS Group

## Theoretical Foundation
The BMS (Bondi-Metzner-Sachs) group is the asymptotic symmetry group of asymptotically flat spacetimes at null infinity. Originally expected to be the 10-parameter Poincaré group, it was discovered to be an infinite-dimensional group. It contains an infinite number of "supertranslations," which are angle-dependent translations of the retarded time. Modern extensions also include "superrotations," which are related to Virasoro symmetries on the celestial sphere.

## Mathematical Formulation
The BMS group is the semi-direct product of the Lorentz group (or its extension) and the infinite-dimensional abelian group of supertranslations $S$:
$$ G_{BMS} = L \ltimes S $$
A supertranslation generator $\xi_f$ is given by:
$$ \xi_f = f(z, \bar{z}) \partial_u $$
where $f(z, \bar{z})$ is any smooth function on the sphere. The commutation relations show that supertranslations don't commute with Lorentz generators, leading to a complex vacuum structure where the "vacuum" is shifted by the passage of radiation.

## Physical Significance & Holography
The BMS group is the symmetry group of the [[celestial-holography|celestial CFT]]. It provides the theoretical foundation for [[black-hole-soft-hair]] and [[gravitational-memory]]. The existence of an infinite number of conserved charges (BMS charges) implies that information about the scattering process is encoded in the asymptotic state of the gravitational field. This group is the key to reconciling the soft infrared structure of gravity with the high-energy scattering of particles.
