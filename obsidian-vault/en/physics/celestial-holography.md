# Celestial Holography

## Theoretical Foundation
Celestial Holography is a dual description of quantum gravity in 4D asymptotically flat spacetimes in terms of a 2D Conformal Field Theory (CFT) living on the "celestial sphere" at null infinity. Unlike the AdS/CFT correspondence, which works for negatively curved spaces, celestial holography aims to describe the physics of our universe. It maps the 4D S-matrix of bulk scattering to 2D correlation functions of operators on the sphere, where the Lorentz group $SO(3,1)$ acts as the global conformal group $SL(2, \mathbb{C})$.

## Mathematical Formulation
The core of the celestial dictionary is the Mellin transform, which maps the energy $\omega$ of a bulk particle to the conformal dimension $\Delta$ of a celestial operator $O_\Delta$:
$$ \tilde{\mathcal{M}}(\Delta, z, \bar{z}) = \int_0^\infty d\omega \, \omega^{\Delta-1} \mathcal{M}(\omega, z, \bar{z}) $$
This transform takes states from the momentum basis (plane waves) to the "conformal primary" basis. In this basis, [[soft-theorems]] appear as the current algebra of the celestial CFT, with the [[bms-group]] generators acting as the symmetry transformations of the 2D theory.

## Physical Significance & Information Theory
Celestial holography provides a new window into the Black Hole Information Paradox and the infrared structure of gravity. It suggests that the holographic degrees of freedom of a flat universe are distributed on the light-like boundary of spacetime. By identifying the 2D CFT dual, researchers hope to calculate quantum gravitational effects and understand how the infinite set of BMS charges preserves information across the scattering process, potentially realizing the "holographic principle" in a flat spacetime setting.
