# Quantum Information and Von Neumann [[gravitational-entropy|Entropy]]

Quantum Information Theory is the study of how information is stored, processed, and transmitted using quantum systems. It bridges the gap between **Quantum Mechanics**, **Information Theory**, and **Thermodynamics**. The central tool in this field is the **Von Neumann Entropy**, which measures the "quantumness" of a state and its entanglement with the environment.

## 1. The Density Matrix ($\rho$) and Purity

In classical probability, we have a distribution $p_i$. In quantum mechanics, we describe an ensemble of states using the **Density Matrix**:
$$\rho = \sum_i p_i |\psi_i\rangle \langle \psi_i|$$

- **Pure States**: If the system is perfectly isolated and in a known state $|\psi\rangle$, then $\rho = |\psi\rangle \langle \psi|$ and $\text{Tr}(\rho^2) = 1$.
- **Mixed States**: If the system is entangled with an unknown environment or is part of a statistical ensemble, $\text{Tr}(\rho^2) < 1$. The "Purity" measures how much information we have preserved.

## 2. Von Neumann Entropy ($S$)

The Von Neumann entropy generalizes [[shannon-entropy|Shannon entropy]] to the quantum domain:
$$S(\rho) = -\text{Tr}(\rho \ln \rho)$$
In the basis where $\rho$ is diagonal (with [[spectral-theory-operators|eigenvalues]] $\lambda_i$), this becomes the familiar $-\sum \lambda_i \ln \lambda_i$.

### Key Mathematical Axioms:
- **Concavity**: $S(\sum w_i \rho_i) \geq \sum w_i S(\rho_i)$. Mixing states always increases uncertainty.
- **Strong Subadditivity**: $S(A,B,C) + S(B) \leq S(A,B) + S(B,C)$. This is the most profound theorem in the field, proving that information shared between three quantum systems is bounded. It is essential for proving the stability of matter.

## 3. Entanglement Entropy: Measuring "Spooky" Links

If a global system $AB$ is in a **Pure State** ($\Psi_{AB}$), its total entropy is zero ($S_{AB} = 0$).
However, if $A$ and $B$ are entangled, the **Reduced Density Matrix** of $A$ ($\rho_A = \text{Tr}_B \rho_{AB}$) will be in a **Mixed State**.

The entropy $S(\rho_A)$ is called the **Entanglement Entropy**. It is a direct physical measure of how many "Ebits" of correlation exist between $A$ and $B$. This is the basis of:
- **Quantum Teleportation**: Sending information by consuming Ebits.
- **Holography**: In [[ads-cft-deep|AdS/CFT]], $S(\rho_A)$ is equal to the area of a minimal surface in a higher-dimensional gravity theory (the Ryu-Takayanagi formula).

## 4. Quantum Communication Limits

- **Holevo's Bound**: Even though a qubit lives in an infinite-dimensional Hilbert space, you can only extract **1 bit** of classical information from it. This limit prevents us from "cheating" classical bandwidth limits using simple quantum states.
- **Data Processing Inequality**: In a quantum channel, the mutual information $I(A:B)$ can never increase. Information once lost to noise can never be recovered by local operations.

## 5. The Thermodynamics of Information

The Von Neumann entropy links information to heat through **Landauer's Principle**: 
*Erasing 1 bit of information (reducing entropy) requires dissipating at least $k_B T \ln 2$ of heat into the environment.*
In modern computing, as chips get smaller, the heat generated by "information erasure" becomes a physical limit to Moore's Law. Quantum computers aim to be **reversible**, avoiding this heat generation entirely.
